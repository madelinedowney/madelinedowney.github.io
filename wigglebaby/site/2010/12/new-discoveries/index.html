New Discoveries

December 8, 2010 at 10:23 pm · Filed under Rants

Greetings, fellow Earthlings, both phosphorous and arsenic-based! Now that I have achieved my triple missions of finding alien life forms, releasing secret government documents, and using the potty, I’m back and ready to tell you all about it.

I’ve had some incredible adventures over the last several months — I traveled to several new places, met countless kitties, puppies and fishies, enjoyed numerous zoos, and experienced the wonders of holidays and snow. Along the way I picked up some skills, obsessions, and silly phrases.

New Skills

My dad and I go to Meijer a lot on the weekends, because mom needs to sleep in. Plus we’re probably out of butter, since I find it necessary to eat butter directly from the jar with a spoon. Anyway, I’ve decided that I’m employed at Meijer now, so here’s some of the skills I listed on my résumé; take note, college grads!

  1. Keeping a “close eye” on an item by moving my face within the immediate proximity of said item.
  2. Building towers with magnet tiles and then subsequently destroying them, using the contradictory practices of Zen non-attachment and destructive arm flailing.
  3. Navigating an iPhone with the prowess of a New York hipster (but absent the beards and poorly conceived irony.)
  4. Asserting my independence by noticeably objecting to wintertime attire of any sort.
  5. Counting, identifying numbers and letters, drawing, painting, and saying goofy words like “rhinoceros,” “jellybeans” and “stinkypants.”
  6. Swimming in hotel pools — evidently hotels are the only places with aquatic facilities.
  7. Spotting spiders from 30 feet away, and demanding that a cleanup action be taken at once!
  8. Clever naming: I have coined many excellent names for my various inanimate friends. Examples: Opie and Eight (dolls), Alli (stuffed cat), Avery (weird jumpy lumberjack toy from a Happy Meal), Peter (any computer), Bubby The P.U. Fish (a smelly plastic bath toy.)
  9. Diaper-free life: I decided to ditch the diapers once and for all, and use the potty full-time. Sure, it’s nice not to pee in one’s pants, but my primary motivation was to sit in the bathroom at length and put stickers all over everything.

New Obsessions

What can I say? I get hooked on stuff.

  1. The Funny Dude: our neighbors put out a motorized robot corpse for Halloween, and after naming it “The Funny Dude” I became incredibly fascinated with him. So much so that I was still requesting access to The Funny Dude well after he was stowed away and the local kids’ Halloween cavities were filled.
  2. Planes, barking dogs, and The Moon: if I hear any noise or see any sky-based objects outside, I demand to see it! I demand satisfaction! This is entirely problematic at 3:00am — Ed.
  3. Tiny things: little stuff is the best. I seek out the smallest individual squares from our various puzzle board games. I prefer the wee baby in my Russian nesting doll set. During my frequent excursions (shifts) at Meijer, I pick up miniature stuffed animals and often convince dad to bring one of them home, then I insist on having all of them in my bed at night. And finally, I love dominos — both for stacking and holding them to my ear, like tiny domino telephones.

New Silly Phrases

There are almost too many to count, but I need to work on counting anyway. 1, 3, 6, 7…

  1. I add “eeyoo!” to the end of people’s names. Examples: Mommyoo, Daddyoo, Maddieyoo, Debbieyoo, etc.
  2. I like to yell for my parents by their real names. “KATE!”
  3. I demonstrate my resolute independence by responding, “no, fine” any time you try to help me with something, particularly when meals, clothing or toothbrushing are involved.
  4. Also on the clothing front, almost all clothes are “too tight” even when they are sweatpants.
  5. When something is of grave concern, such as the noise made by a leaky bathtub drain, I become quite worried and whine, “don’t like it!”
  6. I’m pretty clever, but sometimes I don’t understand what you’re going on about. In those cases, I simply respond “yeah” and hope you won’t notice. For example: “Maddie, what’s the name of that Orangutan?” “Yeah.”
  7. I have a penchant for funny talking, which is exactly like regular talking, except lower and more drawn out. KAYWTEE
  8. It is extremely difficult to record me via standard photographic means due to my insistence upon watching the recording you didn’t get to take. I request this by stating, “I see I see I see!”

Until next time, Peops!
